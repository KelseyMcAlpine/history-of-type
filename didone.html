History of  Typeface Design

❬               Didone               ❭

(1700–1800)


The term Didone is a combination of the names “Bodoni.” and “Didot.” Didone typefaces were inspired by Transitional typefaces, especially Baskerville. Didone typefaces exaggerate the key features of Transitional typefaces: the letters are drawn with a vertical stress and uniform widths (modern proportions), as well as high contrast. One difference from Transitional typefaces, however, is that Didone typefaces do not have bracketed serifs and the terminals become more round rather than lachrymal. Robert Bringhurst attributes this transition to the shift from the broad nib pen to the flexible quill.

Bodoni
