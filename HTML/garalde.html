History of  Typeface Design

❬               Garalde               ❭

(1600–1700)


Garalde typefaces were designed in the 1600s by type masters such as the French printer Claude Garamond and the Venetian printer Aldus Manutius. Typefaces of the Garalde classification show more contrast between thick and thin strokes than Venetian typefaces. Other characteristics of Garalde typefaces include a horizontal e crossbar, bracketed serifs, humanist axis, and lachrymal terminals. Additionally, the letters have wide and rounded apertures which makes them extremely legible. This makes Garalde typefaces perfect for large amounts of text, such as books.

Goudy Old Style


W. Goudy designed Goudy Old Style in 1915. Goudy was an experienced typographer and Goudy Old style was his twenty-fifth typeface. However, this was his first typeface for the American Type Founders. It is easily recognized by the diamond shaped dots on the i, j, and on punctuation. Other recognizable characteristics of Goudy Old Style include the upturned ear of the g; and the curvilinear base of the uppercase E and L.
